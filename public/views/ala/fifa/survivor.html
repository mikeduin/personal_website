The concept of World Cup Survivor mirrors that of its NFL cousin: each entrant must select a single team for each day of the tournament. If that team loses, you are knocked out of the pool; if your team wins or draws, you move on to the next day and pick again. Once an entrant selects a team, that same team cannot be selected again until after World Cup pool play concludes and the top two teams from each pool advance into bracket play, at which point remaining entrants' selections are reset.

The 2014 ALA World Cup Survivor Pool was a $30 buy-in game of 39 entrants; after 33 of those entrants were knocked out on the second day of the tournament when Costa Rica scored an enormous upset of Uruguay, 28 of those entrants ponied up another $30 and started over in a second pool.
